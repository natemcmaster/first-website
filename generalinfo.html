School Hours - back to top

School hours are from 7:50 A.M. to 2:30 P.M.  Students entering the building prior to 7:30 A.M. are to remain in the front lobby until the bell sounds.  We strongly encourage parents not to drop their students off before 7:30 A.M. since there is no teacher supervision.  Students not involved in a supervised after-school activity are to leave the building by 2:45 P.M.

Attendance- back to top

Absences From School

Satisfactory progress in school depends to a large extent on regular attendance.  Absences create problems for both students and teachers.  Students who are frequently absent get behind in their schoolwork and tend to lose interest in school.

All absences are to be reported to the attendance office at 285-8590 each day that your child will be absent.  You are asked to leave a message on voice mail between the hours of 3:30 P.M. and 7:30 A.M.  If a message is not left, a staff member will attempt to contact parents/guardians at home or at work.  If the parent/guardian does not call in and contact is not made, the student must bring a note stating the dates of the absence and reason.  The note should be brought to the office prior to school starting in the morning.  If parent/guardian contact is not made, the absences will be recorded as unapproved.

Excused absences are given for the following reasons:  illness or injury, illness or death in the family, medical and dental appointments, and family trips.  Pre-excuses for anticipated absences are to be requested in writing as far in advance as possible.  For such an absence, a pre-excuse slip is to be obtained from the office in the morning before school and presented to the teacher.  Make-up work is to be requested at this time.  If your student is absent for more than 7 days during the school year because of illness, please provide a note from your physician documenting the nature of the illness.

According to Minnesota statute (260.015), youths under 18 years of age must be in attendance at school.  If a student does not have lawful permission for being absent, that student will be considered truant from school.  A student who has seven or more occurrences of unexcused absences will be considered truant in accordance with state statute.  Action taken by the school will include filing a truancy petition with the county court of the student's residence.  Excessive absences, even with a parental excuse, may result in detention, suspension, or the filing of a truancy petition in district court as required by Minnesota law.

Kellogg is a closed campus building.  Students must have one of the above mentioned parent/guardian pre-approved excuses to leave the building.

If a student becomes ill during the schoo day, he/she is to report to the health office where arrangements will be made for leaving school.  Students are to call their parent/guardian from the nurse's office, not the classroom.

Tardiness

When students arrive late in the morning, they are to report to the main office for an admit slip.  When a student is tardy for school for the third, fourth, and fifth time an in school detention is assigned.  On the sixth tardy a full day of in school suspension will be assigned.  Consequences for tardies to class are determined by the individual teacher or teaching team.

Please remember that coming to class late is a distraction for both the teacher and the class.  Being on time to school is a very important habit to develop.

Make-Up Work For Absences

The first step in requesting make-up work is to obtain your pre-excuse form from the attendance office.  After receiving this form, student may then request make-up homework by presenting this form to their teacher.  The teacher will determine if work is to be made up before or after the absence.  In case of an illness that extends three days or more, parents may request assignments from their child's teachers.  It is expected that assignments will be available for the parent to pick up at school 24 hours after the request is made.  Two days for each day absent is normally the time recommended for make-up work to be completed.  Please call the counseling office at 285-8707 to request make-up work for unanticipated absences.

Teams- back to top

All sixth, seventh, and eight grade students are placed on a team.  The sixth grade will consist of the yellow, orange, and gold teams, and purple Core Knowledge teams; the seventh grade will consist of the blue and silver teams; the eighth grade will consist of the green and red teams.  Each team has its own schedule, policies, and procedures, with teams often planning their own activities independent of each other.  Due to scheduling difficulties, requests for specific teams/teachers are not permitted.

Time Schedule- back to top

Seventh and eighth grade classes will operate on a seven-period schedule.  Each team will be assigned a lunch which will be printed at the top of the student's schedule.  The sixth grade will operate on a seven-period day in a predominantyl self-contained classroom.  Precise schedules will be determined by the sixth grade staff.

Student Information Card- back to top

A student information card is sent home with students on the first day of school.  Please see that this card is completed by parent/guardian and returned to school as soon as possible.  A field trip authorization is on the card.  No student will be allowed to go on a field trip if the card is not signed by the parent/guardian and returned to the student's homeroom teacher.

Hall Passes- back to top

All students are expected to be in class at the end of passing time.  No students should be in the halls or restrooms during class time without a hall pass.

Lockers- back to top

Students are assigned a hall locker for their books, coats, and supplies.  Students are also issued a physical education locker.  It is the student's responsibility to keep the combination a secret.  Students should not share their locker or combination with anyone other than their locker mate!

Lockers are the property of the school and can be opened for inspection if it is reasonably suspected that the contents might be in violation of school rules.

If a locker is not working properly, please notify the main office immediately.

Cafeteria and School Lunch- back to top

A variety of main dishes, salads, fruits, and desserts are provided as part of the school lunch program each day.  Students will have their own individual lunch account into which they will deposit money.  Students may also bring a cold lunch from home.

Each student is to contribute to a pleasant atmosphere while eating in the cafeteria.  Students are expected to sit at their assigned tables in the section designated for their grade level.  Tables are to be left neat and clean for the next group of students.  Trays are to be returned and students are dismissed at the direction of the cafeteria supervisors.

Physical Education Uniforms- back to top

A uniform is required at the middle school level for physical education.  Uniforms are available for a nominal fee at Adlers Sporting Goods.

Family Status Change- back to top

Please notify the office if there is a change of family address or telephone number.  Also, notify the school counselor if there is a separation, divorce, or change in guardian or custody arrangements.

Textbooks- back to top

The school provides textbooks for every student at no cost.  Students are to care for the books in a responsible manner to make it possible for books to be used over a normal period of time.  Students are required to have a book cover on all texts.  Fines will be levied for damage or loss of books.  The student's name, grade and room number should be recorded in the space provided in each textbook so it can be readily identified and returned if misplaced.

Target/Advisory Program- back to top

Part of the middle school philosophy includes the development of a target/advisor/advisee program in which all students will participate. 

This program attempts to match each student with an adult to whom they may address concerns or questions.  The program also addresses areas that relate to the child's social, emotional, and academic development.  This hour of class time is also used as a study period.

Parent/Teacher Conferences- back to top

Conferences are held twice during the year to provide parents/guardians with information about their child's academic performance.  It is also an opportunity to learn more about the programs for the students at the middle school level.  Parents/guardians are encouraged, however, to contact the school any time they have a concern.

Media Center- back to top

The media center is open from 7:30 A.M. to 2:45 P.M.  Students may visit the media center before school, during their target class, and after school.  Many English teachers also schedule a regular book check out time.  Books are checked out for 14 days and may be renewed.  ID's are required for book check out and computer use.  The Media Center provides many book and online resources.

Parent/Guardian Involvement- back to top

The Kellogg staff welcomes and encourages involvement from the parents/guardians of our students.  The participation of parents/guardians in all aspects of their student's school experience can lead to positive and rewarding benefits for both the child and adult.  A monthly newsletter is sent home with students to provide information to parents/guardians about the Kellogg Middle School program.

PTSA- back to top

The PTSA is a parent/teacher/student directed organization that operates to encourage parent and public involvement in school.  PTSA programs are involved in the following:

  • Promotes awareness of local school district and broader legislative issues.

  • Provides opportunities for parent/teacher/student interaction within the school.

  • Promotes and supports positive parenting skills.

  • Secures quality education and lives for children.

Volunteers in Education (VIE)- back to top

The Volunteers in Education program is a staff-directed program that coordinates volunteer work in the schools.  Volunteers help children learn either directly in the classroom and media center or indirectly by working with staff so they have more time for the students.

Visitors- back to top

All visitors must report to the main office when entering the building.  Students who wish to bring a guest must obtain permission at least three days in advance of the visitation day.  A note from the parent approving the visit is to be circulated to teachers for their notification and approval.  No visitors will be allowed during the first week of school and the last month of school.  Students from other Rochester schools are not permitted to be guests at Kellogg, nor are students from area schools when their school is in session.

Activity Fee/Fundraising- back to top

The purpose of charging an activity fee is to reduce the number of fundraisers at Kellogg.  Students will not be charged an activity fee for educational experiences that are written into the district's curriculum.  However, each team of teachers will be sending a letter home in the fall requesting an activity fee from each student to be used for educational activities  not written into the district's curriculum.  The letter will state the amount and the purpose of the money.  No student will be denied any of the activities due to economic hardships.  Families that cannot afford all or part of the activity fee will have all or the remainder of the fee picked up by the Kellogg PTSA or the Kellogg Student Council.  Please contact the main office or your child's Target teacher to obtain a waiver of the activity fee.  The activity fee may also be paid in installments throughout the school year.  This fee will not be reimbursed except for health issues, major family emergencies, or the student's relocation.

Student Services- back to top

The school district provides a number of extended services to students who may need additional assistance to succeed in school.  Some of those services that are available at Kellogg are:

School Guidance Counselors

Counseling and guidance services are available to all students.  Theses services include assistance with educational planning, help with scheduling difficulties, interpretation of standard test scores, occupational and career information and help with personal concerns.  Students who wish to see their counselor may make an appointment before school.

School Social Worker

The school social worker provides assistance to students who have personal or family situations that may interfere with their ability to attend, achieve, or adjust to school.  The social worker meets with students individually or in support groups, and may make referrals to community agencies as needed.

School Psychologist

Our school psychologist works with students, teachers, parents, community agencies and the school system to meet the unique needs of learners.  School psychologists are specialists in the gathering, interpretation and administration of data that is crucial in assisting school staff and parent(s) to meet a child's unique needs.

Health Staff

Kellogg has a health office staffed full-time by a health secretary and part-time by a school nurse.  STudents who become ill or are injured during the school day are to report to the health office.  Health records are kept in this office and parents/guardians are requested to update this information relative to medical conditions.  All medications are to be kept and administered in the health office.  Please see the "medication policy" that follows.

Medication Policy- back to top

Both prescription medications and over-the-counter medications are to be kept in the health office in their original containers.  Medication to be administered to a student during the school day must be brought to school by the parent/guardian.  Both non-prescription medications and prescription medications can only be administered to a student if the school is provided with authorization.  The medication administration form must be signed by the parent for over-the-counter medications; both a parent and a physicians signature is required for prescription medications.

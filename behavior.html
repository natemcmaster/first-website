Student Conduct and Expectations - back to top

The general expectation for behavior is that everyone, students and staff, treat one another with mutual respect and dignity.  Students are to engage in behavior that contributes to a positive learning environment for everyone.  The expectations for student behavior at Kellogg Middle School follows.  Students will:

  1. Demonstrate respect for all people.

  2. Use language free of profanity, ethnic slurs, and name-calling.

  3. Show respect for the property of others.

  4. Be in their assigned areas on time.

  5. Help keep the entire campus clean.

  6. Demonstrate appropriate behavior.

  7. Expect recognition and reward for work well done.

  8. Dress with respect for self and others.

  9. Follow building-wide behavior management plan for the common areas.

  10. Follow all school, cafeteria, and classroom rules.

Harassment- back to top

A student who engages in verbal, written, or physical harassment will be subject to disciplinary actions such as detention, out-of-school suspension, and parent conference.  Harassment is defined as but is not limited to the following acts:

  1. Offensive or objectionable to the recipient.

  2. Causes the recipient discomfort or humiliation

  3. Interferes with the recipient's learning.

  4. creates a hostile or intimidating learning environment.

  5. Believed by the recipient or observer to be demeaning or discriminatory toward the sex, race, religion, national origin of an individual or groups of individuals.

Receiving Direction From School Personnel- back to top

Any expression of disrespect, refusal to respond, or abusive or threatening language directed toward any staff member will not be tolerated.  If, in the course of performing his/her professional duty, a staff member (including substitues) encounters such behavior, the student will be subject to the following consequences:

  1. Detention

  2. Restriction of privileges

  3. Parent/Guardian conference

  4. Out-of-school suspension

  5. Expulsion

Fighting- back to top

A student who engages in pushing, shoving or other bully-type behavior leading to a potential fight could be subject to detention or out-of-school suspension.

A student who engages in fighting could be subject to an out-of-school suspension lasting up to 15 days.  A student who is involved in subsequent fighting incidents will be subject to more severe penalties including additional out-of-school suspensions and potential consideration for expulsion.  Parents/guardians will be requested to attend a re-entry conference.

Weapons- back to top

No student shall knowingly possess, handle, or transport any object that can reasonably be considered a weapon on school property or off school property at any school event.  Violation of the weapons policy will result in immediate suspension out of school and recommendation for expulsion.

Use of Tobacco- back to top

It is illegal in Minnesota for minors to possess or purchase tobacco.  Effective 9/1/87, smoking and the use of tobacco products shall be prohibited on school district property.  This shall include school buildings, grounds, and school-owned vehicles.  Upon first violation of this policy, students will complete a program related to smoking cessation.  The police will also ticket students for possession of tobacco under the legal age beginning with the first offense and also on subsequent offenses.  Further infractions will result in out-of-school suspension and possible expulsion.

Alcohol and Drugs- back to top

A chemical is defined as any alcoholic beverage, narcotic drug, controlled substance, hallucinogenic drug, amphetamine, barbiturate, marijuana, intoxicant of any kind, or any substance represented as the above.  No student is allowed to have an illicit drug in his/her possession on school grounds or off school grounds while the student is participating in a school-sponsored activity.  Such a drug found in a student's possession will be confiscated and turned over to the police liaison officer.  The student will be subject to out-of-school suspension and the initiation of expulsion proceedings for repeated offenses.  It is forbidden for any student to possess dangerous/chemicals or substances purported to be drugs or chemicals with the intent to distribute it to other students.  Recommendation for expulsion is considered for an offense of this nature.

Student Dress- back to top

Students are to be appropriately dressed, neatly groomed and attired in good taste at all times.  Primary guidelines to be considered are the clothing's effect on student safety, student health, and its distraction to other students including implied threats, gang association, and intimidation.  Designs on clothing are to be inoffensive  and appropriate to a school setting.  Inappropriate clothing would include clothes with smoking, alcohol, illegal chemicals or sexual connotations. Clothes should cover undergarments at all times.  Tops with spaghetti straps or halter tops are NOT allowed.  Hats and sunglasses are not to be worn inside the building.  Parents/guardians are asked to assume the responsibility for student guidance in the matter of good grooming and dress.

Personal Electronic Devices- back to top

Cell phones, pagers, walkmans, portable CD and tape players are not allowed to be carried by students.  The above items will be confiscated from students and parents will be asked to pick them up.  Laser pointers will be confiscated and will not be returned to parents or students.  Due to safety issues, students who are in possession of a laser pointer may be suspended for this offense.

Personal Property Theft/Lost and Found- back to top

The school assumes no responsibility for the loss of money or personal property, including items that may be stolen from lockers.  Large sums of money or valuable items should be left in the office in the morning and reclaimed at the end of the day if it is necessary to bring them to school.  Please notify the office immediately of items that are stolen or lost.  Lost and found articles are to be left or recovered in the office.

Damage to Property- back to top

Students who deface or destroy property not belonging to them shall pay for all such damages.  Students may also be assigned to clean-up duty.  Parents/guardians will be notified.  Each student is expected to use his/her books, locker, and other school equipment with care; items belonging to the school should remain free from pencil and ink and all other defacement.

Student Use of Internet Accounts- back to top

All Internet users are expected to abide by the general rules of computer and network etiquette.  Access to the Internet from school district computers is to be for educational purposes only.

The building administrators reserve the right to terminate immediately the account of any student who misuses the Internet.  The building/system administrators will deem what is inappropriate and their decision is final.  No students shal intentionally:

  1. Use the network for any illegal activity, including violation of copyright or other laws.

  2. Access any inappropriate materials that may be on the Internet.  No inappropriate materials may be loaded on the school district workstations or printed form school district printers

  3. Use of the Internet which violates school policies and behavioral standards.

  4. Use the network for financial or commercial gain.

  5. Degrade or disrupt equipment or system performance.

  6. Invade the privacy of other individuals by accessing and/or vandalizing their computerized data.

  7. Waste technology resources, including bandwidth, file space, and printers.

  8. Gain unauthorized access to resources or entities.

  9. Use an account owned by another user, with or without permission.

  10. Post personal communications or an electronic bulletin board without the author's consent.

Students must complete and sign the Internet Contract in order to gain access to the Internet.

Backpacks- back to top

Students may bring backpacks to school; however, backpacks must be stored in lockers to obtain items needed for the next class.

Kellogg Behavior Management Plan For Common Areas- back to top

The Kellogg Behavior Management Plan is designed to be used for behavior in common areas of the school.  All staff members will give out "Stop and Think" tickets for behavior that is inappropriate.

Items on "Stop and Think" tickets include the following:

  1. Pushing/shoving/running

  2. Throwing/shooting/spitting

  3. Gum/Candy/Food/Pop

  4. Disruptive noises

  5. Teasing/name calling

  6. Offensive language

  7. Traveling without a pass

  8. Talk back/refuse to cooperate

  9. Public display of affection

  10. Disrespect to property

Any staff member can give a "Stop and Think" ticket.  The tickets are given to the team and the Target teacher has a short conference with the student regarding the tickets. When the student reaches the number of "Stop and Think" tickets listed below, the following consequences occur:

  • 3 tickets = 2 lunch detentions

  • 5 tickets = 4 lunch detentions

  • 7 tickets = 1 day of BLC and parent conference

  • 9 tickets = 2 days of BLC and Development of Alternatives

PLEASE NOTE:The "Stop and Think" tickets total up over the entire year and do not start over each semester.

"Kool Komet" tickets will be given out for behavior above and beyond what normal students exhibit.  Students fill out the "Kool Komet" tickets and deposit them in a bucket by the media center for a weekly drawing.  Weekly prizes will be given out and, each quarter a drawing will be held for a bigger prize.

Detention- back to top

Students may be assigned to after-school detention by an administrator for failure to follow the rules of Kellogg Middle School.  Detention is held Monday through Thursday from 2:30-3:30 P.M.  Parents/guardians are notified of detention assignment by a note or telephone call.  Students are expected to notify and discuss the issues with their parents.  Rules for detention are as follows:

  1. Be on time

  2. Bring study materials:  books, assignments, paper, etc.

  3. It is expected that students will study during detention

  4. There's no talking or use of walk-mans during detention.

  5. No passes from detention will be issued.

Individual teachers may also assign detention before or after school in their classrooms for infractions of rules occurring in class.  The teacher will notify parents of detention assignments by a note or a telephone call.

Behavioral Learning Center- back to top

The Behavioral Learning Center (BLC) is an in-school suspension program.  The purpose of the program is to assist students in improving their behavior and to provide them with the opportunity to continue to make academic progress through teacher-provided assignments.  It is believed that remaining in school is the best situation for a majority of students, even though they may be having behavior problems.  Therefore, the Behavioral Learning Center is a positive alternative to out-of-school suspension.

Suspension- back to top

If a student is suspended from school for a day or more, he/she is not to be on school property during the school day.  Failure to do so shall constitute a trespass and shall be reported to the appropriate law enforcement official.

Behavioral Contract- back to top

All students and parents/guardians will read, sign, and return the behavioral contract found in the Kellogg Planner or in the main office at Kellogg.  This contract is a tool used to help students and parents/guardians understand the guidelines by which the Kellogg student body conducts themselves in order to maintain the best possible learning environment for Kellogg.
